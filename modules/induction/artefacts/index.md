## Artefact 01 - Induction Diagnostic

### Objective
The purpose of the Induction Diagnostic was to evaluate academic readiness for postgraduate study, including written communication, analytical thinking, reflective ability, and understanding of academic expectations.

### Submission
I completed and submitted the diagnostic as part of the induction phase, using it as an opportunity to demonstrate structured thinking, professional writing ability, and readiness to engage with postgraduate-level study.

### Feedback Received
Tutor feedback highlighted both strengths and opportunities for deeper development.

**Strengths identified**
- strong written communication  
- clear structure and readability  
- good engagement with the task  
- evidence of thoughtful reflection  

**Development opportunities identified**
- greater analytical depth  
- deeper critical engagement with ideas  
- stronger expansion of arguments  
- further development of academic discussion at postgraduate level  

### Learning Taken Forward
This feedback was valuable because it clarified an important distinction:

Postgraduate work is not only about communicating clearly - it is also about demonstrating depth, critique, and academic analysis.

This insight has already influenced how I plan to approach future written work in the programme.

- induction diagnostic submission
